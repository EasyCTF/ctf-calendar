About

As CTFs become more popular, it's important to create a central repository of CTFs. Thus, this site aims to remind CTF participants about upcoming events, as well as help CTF organizers get the word out about their competition.

Ultimately, we may expand this site to include a way to index teams and track their performance, but as of now, it's simply a list of upcoming and archived events. Previously, adding to the calendar required moderated approval through pull requests in the Git repository, so this site will also require all user-submitted entries to be approved before they are added to the main calendar on the front page.

If you are familiar with CTFtime, this site is similar in concept to CTFtime, but targeted at the high school level.

What is CTF?

No, we're not running around in a gym, tagging other players. Capture the flag contests, or CTFs for short, are intense cybersecurity contests where participants try to capture a piece of information. To do this, they must use skills to capture a virtual flag, usually taking the form of a string, like this: flag{this_is_a_flag}. In most jeopardy-style CTFs (like EasyCTF), participants are tested on their security skills in the form of web, binary exploitation, forensics, cryptography, and reverse-engineering challenges.
